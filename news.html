August 2025 · 八月
Dragon Boat Festival Outdoor Outreach

Church website editor Chen Ting reports.

The 26th annual Rhode Island Dragon Boat Festival was held on August 30th at Festival Pier in Pawtucket. This year's festival was larger than last year, attracting over 20 professional and amateur teams. The church's dragon boat team, Row Model, once again sent nearly 25 members to compete.

The church's Chinese and English sections and the Chinese school set up a joint outreach booth at this year's Dragon Boat Festival, offering games suitable for all ages, such as balloon twisting, rock drawing, curling, tic-tac-toe, puzzles, gospel stickers and pens, colorful beads, and gospel crafts. They also distributed a large number of evangelistic pamphlets.

The booth was packed with visitors, especially families and children, who all had a wonderful time. We not only brought joy to the community but also had the opportunity to sow seeds of the gospel.

Thanks be to God, more than 50 brothers and sisters from the Chinese and English churches, as well as 4 friends from the Boston Short-Term Mission Center, participated in the outreach activities that day. Some new friends also came to church for a service the day after the Dragon Boat Festival outreach.

The Gospel is the power of God. This event allowed us to experience God's wonderful protection and grace, enabling us to reach more Chinese people, raising community awareness of the church, and letting more people know about the church and Chinese schools. Glory, praise, and accord to the one true God who loves us.

October 2025 · 十月
Vermont Fall Foliage Trips

Home Fellowship Vermont Fall Foliage Tour (Oct. 10–13)

Contributed by Xiong Fang, editor-in-chief of the church website.

From October 10th to 13th, over forty brothers and sisters from Home Fellowship traveled to Vermont for a four-day fall foliage viewing trip, staying at Sonnets ES Suites in Williston, VT. The trip not only allowed them to appreciate the magnificent autumn scenery but also provided an opportunity to experience the abundant grace of the Lord through fellowship and worship.

On Saturday, everyone went hiking in Mount Philo State Park in Charlotte, VT — a trail of about 1.9 miles. Along the way, the maple forests were layered and a gentle breeze caressed their faces. After reaching the summit, overlooking Lake Champlain and the autumn colors of the mountains, everyone praised the wonder and greatness of God's creation.

That afternoon, the group visited the famous Church Street Marketplace in downtown Burlington, enjoying street music, local delicacies, and ice cream. In the evening, the brothers and sisters boarded the Harvest Moon Dinner Cruise and sailed along Lake Champlain. The sunset and autumn colors intertwined, the lake was as calm as a mirror, and distant lights twinkled.

On Sunday morning, the group drove to the picturesque town of Stowe and attended Sunday worship at the renowned Stowe Community Church, where Brother Paul preached on the theme of "Love and Justice" from Psalm 85:10.

"Behold, how good and pleasant it is when brothers dwell together in unity!" — Psalm 133:1

New Song Canaan Fellowship Vermont Trip (Oct. 11–13)

Contributed by Yunmei, special editor for the church website.

Eight families from New Song Canaan Fellowship — a total of 29 people — also embarked on a Vermont autumn foliage journey. During three days and two nights, they appreciated the beauty of God's creation, trekked through mountains to discover the beauty of waterfalls, showcased their culinary skills in the kitchen, and participated in various games amidst laughter and joy.

God prepared a special place for them to sing and worship freely, with wonderful testimonies and sharing from the brothers and sisters. They happily climbed mountains, worshipped the Lord with sincere hearts, and shared their innermost thoughts during evening tea breaks. The children played joyfully in the water. Every moment spent with family, every gathering in fellowship, allowed them to feel God's grace and presence even more deeply.

Thanks be to the Lord for His provision, and thanks to the brothers and sisters for their thoughtful arrangements. Although the journey is not yet over, they are already looking forward to their next gathering.

November 2025 · 十一月
Thanksgiving, Bible Competition & Joint Fellowship

2025 Church Thanksgiving Activities

Deacon Song Xiaohui reports.

This year's Thanksgiving event was held and concluded successfully under the Lord's grace. Nearly 500 brothers and sisters and guests participated, creating a warm, lively, and joyful atmosphere.

The various fellowships within the church devoted themselves wholeheartedly to the preparations. From setup and reception to catering and the sharing of testimonies, everything proceeded smoothly and collaboratively. The lunch that day was plentiful and delicious, and many longtime friends, thanks to the care and concern of fellowship members, once again deeply felt the warmth of the church family.

The post-meal thanksgiving sharing was even more touching. Brothers and sisters recounted their experiences of God's power and guidance over the past year, testifying to God's grace and giving glory to the true God, filling the entire church with gratitude and joy.

What moved us most was not the increase in numbers, but the willingness of every brother and sister to serve together, allowing love and gratitude to truly flow among us.

The 8th Bible Knowledge Competition (Nov. 9)

Basic Education Department.

On November 9th, the 8th Bible Knowledge Competition was held at the church. This year's competition focused on the Book of Acts, with the Faithful, Good, Song of Canaan, Living Water, and Heavenly Road groups participating. After a friendly 40-minute competition, the Living Water group won first place, while the Heavenly Road and Faithful groups won second and third place respectively.

A new approach was adopted this year: one round of 40 minutes in a semi-open-book format — 25 minutes closed-book, followed by 15 minutes where participants could open their paper Bibles. It is hoped this approach will encourage participation, reduce competition pressure, and allow participants to enjoy the reading process.

The Bible knowledge competition began in 2017 and has been held annually except for 2020 due to the pandemic. May God continue to guide this competition and may our brothers and sisters strive harder to read the Bible.

Jia Mei & Caleb Joint Fellowship — "Regaining Strength"

Contributed by He Xiaomei, Special Editor of the Church Website.

On the third weekend of November, the brothers and sisters of Jia Mei and Caleb Fellowship welcomed their long-awaited joint fellowship. The event was held in the main hall with more than 80 brothers and sisters in attendance, filled with laughter, gentle greetings, and warm interactions.

The event, scheduled from 10:00 AM to 12:00 PM, focused on the theme of renewed strength. It began with praise, as everyone joyfully sang "Soaring on Wings of an Eagle." A missionary couple supported by the church shared their experiences and calling, including their ministry in the Philippines where they serve children of missionary families from around the world.

A sister from the Jia Mei Fellowship shared her experience of battling cancer for over two years, recounting God's grace and His work in her life, how God upheld her with His mighty arm and carried her through the valley of death. She also shared the unwavering care from the brothers and sisters who were more than family to her.

The event concluded with a wonton-making lunch session. The most popular dish was Shanghai-style wontons with shepherd's purse and pork filling, prepared by sisters from Shanghai. Laughter and joy echoed in the dining room.

"But those who hope in the Lord will renew their strength; they will soar on wings of an eagle; they will run and not grow weary, they will walk and not faint." — Isaiah 40:31
December 2025 · 十二月
Christmas Season: Love in Action

Visit to Cranston Chinese Seniors Activity Center (Dec. 29)

Church website editor Chen Ting reports.

On December 29th, the Ministry of Evangelism organized its first visit to the Cranston Chinese Day Care Center for Seniors. The church sent 17 brothers and sisters and their children, and 7 friends from the Boston Short-Term Mission Center also came to offer support.

Everyone came to serve with excitement and anticipation. The children opened the event with "Jesus Loves Me," and then parents and children danced the Hawaiian hula. Pastor Zeng Shizeng had a relaxed conversation with the elders in fluent Mandarin and Cantonese, reminding them that the most important purpose of Christmas is to share the love of Christ. We also brought Christmas gift bags to forty elders.

A special activity had participants make "eight-colored fish" crafts with the elderly, each color sharing the gospel message — from God's creation (green), to humanity's separation from God (black), to Christ's redemption (red), new life (white), joy and peace (yellow), salvation (blue), reconciliation (purple), and growth in faith (orange).

English Class Christmas Celebration (Dec. 21)

On Sunday, December 21st, the English class held a Christmas celebration, attracting over 20 students, family members, new friends, teachers, and teaching assistants. The celebration included Christmas carols, delicious food, Christmas games, and a gift exchange.

Pastor Zeng Shizeng and his wife were invited to share the meaning of Christmas, encouraging everyone to emulate the selflessness and giving of Jesus Christ. Sister Hou Ya-ying, a student in the English class, shared her touching testimony of salvation — she saw a gentle, unseen hand guiding her to church, accepted salvation in faith, and became a member of the church last Christmas.

Christmas Charity Concert — Wakefield Concert Band (Dec. 14)

The concert attracted approximately 400 community members. The Wakefield Concert Band performed several classic Christmas carols, culminating in a joyful sing-along. Participants dressed in Christmas costumes, and the stage was decorated with poinsettias, filling the entire church with a festive atmosphere. The concert also raised funds for the Elisha Project and Central Falls Library.

Brown University Shooting — Church Response

Following the incident, the church acted swiftly: preparing Chinese meals to comfort affected Chinese students and their families, arranging brothers and sisters to deliver meals and pick up students to the airport, and Pastor Zeng visiting and praying for patients at the hospital.

"We are truly grateful to have someone by our side and to show us care during this time." — Parents of affected students
"You are angels on earth, guardians sent by God!" — Community response

Christmas Love Lunch Delivery (Dec. 20)

The church, in conjunction with the Elisha Project, distributed Christmas gift packages and Chinese lunches to 500 families in need. Brothers and sisters began shopping, organizing, and preparing on Thursday. The stove was lit at 5:30 a.m. on Saturday morning. They worked non-stop for four hours and completed 550 boxed meals — pan-fried dumplings, char siu, pork ribs, vermicelli, and cabbage, with a bowl of white rice.

"God is love, and whoever lives in love lives in God, and God in him." — 1 John 4:16

A Gift — Reflection by Jia Mei Fellowship (Dec. 7)

On December 7th, the Jia Mei Fellowship sang "A Gift" and "Amazing Grace" during worship. The familiar melodies filled the hall, gentle and sincere, reminding us that God has already prepared the best gift for humanity — Jesus Christ. Gifts are not earned through merit; they come from a loving heart and are given freely and unreservedly. More than two thousand years ago, God prepared a grace more precious and profound than any other gift in the world.

Christmas reminds us that what is most precious to us is not what we can give to God, but what God has already given us — He gave Jesus to the world, brought light into darkness, placed hope in brokenness, and put eternity into the finite.

Reported by Yang Minli.

January 2024 · 一月
"May Your Will Be Done" — Year-End Reflection

Scripture: Matthew 6:9–13

The theme of our church in 2023 was "May Your will be done." Every Sunday's sermon throughout the year revolved around this theme. As the year closes, it is fitting to reflect on what God has called us to through each month:

January — fellowship; February — love everyone; March — worship Him; April — be His disciples; May — serve; June — obey; July — dedicate ourselves; August — become His children; September — uphold our faith; October — share the gospel; November — be equipped; December — be with Him in Heaven.

A. Understanding God's Will

There are 74 Bible verses related to "God's will" — 16 in the Old Testament and 58 in the New Testament. God acts according to His own will (Dan 4:35); His will is irresistible (Rom 9:19) and unchangeable (Heb 6:17). He ordained His will before the ages (Eph 2:11), and all things were created by His will (Rev 4:11). Christ sacrificed Himself according to the Father's will to redeem the sinful world (Gal 1:4).

B. The Requirements of God's Will

We are called to pray "May your will be done" (Matt 6:10), to willingly do God's will (Ps 40:8), to understand the Lord's will (Eph 5:17), to serve from the heart (Eph 6:6), and to give thanks in all circumstances (1 Thess 5:18). God's will is for people to be sanctified, to do good, and to shepherd willingly and joyfully (1 Pet 5:2).

C. The Efficacy of Understanding God's Will

God hears the prayers of those who obey His will (John 9:31). In all things, God works together for good to those who love Him (Rom 8:28). God has predestined people to be an inheritance according to His will (Eph 1:11). Whoever obeys God's will will live forever (1 John 2:17), and only by doing His will can one enter the Kingdom of Heaven (Matt 7:21).

D. Why God's Will Is Sometimes Missed

Man can obscure God's will with ignorant words (Job 38:2); some despise it (Ps 107:11); others reject it for their own opinions (Luke 7:30); still others resist or withhold it (Ps 2:2; Acts 20:27).

E. How to Understand God's Will

Be instructed in the Word (Rom 2:18). Offer your body as a living sacrifice, renewing your mind (Rom 12:1–2): "Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind. Then you will be able to test and approve what God's will is — his good, pleasing and perfect will." Resolve to do His will (John 7:17). Be filled with the Holy Spirit (Eph 5:17–18).

November 2024 · 十一月
Thanksgiving: Testimonies, Feast & "Lift Up the Cup"

Sister Zhou Can's Thanksgiving Testimony

Three years ago, I changed to a new psychologist, who decided to stop prescribing sleeping pills. Initially, I was very worried, having taken medication for over ten years. At my worst, I couldn't sleep for four consecutive nights, had severe headaches, felt mentally disoriented, and even contemplated suicide. So I prayed to God the Father: "Dear Abba Father, please forgive my sins... Please grant me a peaceful night and a good night's sleep." After praying, that night God granted me five hours of sleep — something I had never imagined. The first thing I did the next morning was to thank the Lord, praise and glorify His power, and give glory to God the Father.

My sleep has improved since then, and I have never used sleeping pills again, completely freeing myself from Satan's bondage.

"Blessed is the Lord, for he has heard my cry for help. The Lord is my strength and my shield; in him I trust and am helped." — Psalm 28:6–7
"Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God. And the peace of God, which transcends all understanding, will guard your hearts and your minds in Christ Jesus." — Philippians 4:6–7

A Sister's Testimony — God's Presence

During two months of agonizing insomnia, unable to get help from my family doctor and struggling to obtain medication, I never stopped praying to God. I knew God was with me and that I should trust in Him, but I was still afraid. One early morning, I even had the thought of driving out to end my life — yet at that very moment, the Holy Spirit did not abandon me. A voice told me that my Lord would be with me in times of trouble and that I should cling tightly to Him. Without realizing it, I said: "I completely trust in You, Lord; I will not fear in God's presence!"

With the care and help of my daughter and son-in-law, I found a psychologist, and after trying many different medications, we finally found one that worked. In the prayers of brothers and sisters in Christ, and the care and love of my family, I experienced the healing hand of God. How we relied on God to heal these wounds — and were transformed and set free through Jesus — can also help others be saved and transformed, to have eternal life and hope.
